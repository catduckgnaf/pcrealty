What Makes New England So Wicked?

Discover the charm, history, and vibrant future of the Hartford-Springfield region.

First Thing's First: What Does "Wicked" Mean?

If you spend any time in Massachusetts or greater New England, you'll hear the word "wicked." Don't worry, it's a good thing! Originating as slang in the Boston area, "wicked" is a unique regional adverb that simply means "very" or "extremely." Saying something is "wicked awesome" is the highest form of praise. It's a term of endearment that captures the uniquely fantastic character of our region, and we think you'll agree that life and opportunities here are, in fact, wicked good.

The Knowledge Corridor: A Hub of Opportunity

The Hartford-Springfield region is the cultural and economic heart of New England, often referred to as the "Knowledge Corridor." It's the second-largest metropolitan area in New England, boasting a dense concentration of world-class universities, innovative companies, and a rich history. Its strategic location, nestled between Boston and New York City, provides incredible access to major markets while maintaining a distinct and affordable quality of life.

Highlights of the Hartford-Springfield Area

Attractions in Springfield, MA

  • The MGM Springfield: A world-class destination for entertainment, dining, and gaming, revitalizing the downtown core.
  • Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame: An international shrine to the sport of basketball, which was invented right here in Springfield.
  • The Amazing World of Dr. Seuss Museum: A celebration of the beloved author Theodor Geisel, a Springfield native.
  • Forest Park: One of the largest urban parks in the country, featuring a zoo, gardens, athletic fields, and miles of trails.
  • The Big E: New England's largest fair, a massive annual event celebrating the agriculture and culture of the six New England states.

Attractions in Hartford, CT

  • The Mark Twain House & Museum: Explore the stunning home where the famous author wrote his most iconic works.
  • Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art: The oldest continuously-operating public art museum in the United States.
  • The Bushnell Performing Arts Center: A premier venue for Broadway shows, concerts, and cultural events.
  • Elizabeth Park Conservancy: Home to the country's oldest public rose garden, a beautiful spot for recreation and relaxation.

A Center for Education and Innovation

The region's identity is deeply tied to its academic institutions, which drive innovation and provide a highly educated workforce. Major universities and colleges include:

  • University of Massachusetts Amherst
  • Smith College
  • Mount Holyoke College
  • Amherst College
  • Hampshire College
  • Trinity College
  • Western New England University

This academic environment fosters a vibrant culture and presents significant opportunities for real estate investors, particularly in the rental market.
